My Journey To Here

About

I've always been into technology. I'm your regular enthusiast when it comes to computers and electronics of any kind. I love watching, helping, and being there as technology grows. I have several computers and a few old rack-serves that I work on as a hobby. Along with hanging out with friends, most of my free time is spent tinkering and trying out different things. My more recent projects have been/are setting up a local data base and learning how to run a website, C#/Node.js server and data bae all on my local systems. I don't have the technology to properly setup security, so I'll probably just settle with running it locally for right now.

Brief Recap of Work History

I was privileged enough to get a job right after I turned 16. I was able to land the job at the Jack in The Box between my house and the high school I was in thanks to the help of a friend at the time. I grew to really like the team I worked with at the time. Though for a time, as it goes with fast food, the management was replaced with someone who wasn't as good as the last. During this time the management was bad enough for me to pick up a second job at Dominos for about 8 months. At the start of that 8 months I was still in school, so managing both was a little difficult, but eventually I was able to schedule one in the morning and the other in the afternoon. In the last half of the 14 months I worked at Jack in The Box I started taking on responsibilities of the team leads and even the GM when it came to inventory. It went downhill very quickly as the now new General Manager (GM) didn't see me for any of that. And only as the minor who was 50-60 hours a week. Which understandable was a liability for her. As it was technically illegal for me to be working that much. But even then, I had bills to pay. So, following my two weeks and resignation, a received a job offer at taco bell. I worked there for about 4 months before leaving there for a better opportunity at Sevy Brothers Construction. There I worked with literally all 5 of my roommates and ~6 other friends and family of theirs, until I had to leave there for health reasons nearly four months later. Which is when started into BoiseCodeCamp, bringing me here to you.
